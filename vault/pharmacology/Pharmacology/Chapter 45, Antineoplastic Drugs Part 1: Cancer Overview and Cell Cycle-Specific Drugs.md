# Chapter 45, Antineoplastic Drugs Part 1: Cancer Overview and Cell Cycle-Specific Drugs

To master the content of this chapter, a student must understand the biological basis of cancer, the specific mechanisms of cell cycle-specific drugs, and the complex nursing management required for patients undergoing chemotherapy.

### Main Points for Mastery

#### 1. Cancer Fundamentals and Classification

* **Tumor Types:** Distinguish between benign (well-defined, encapsulated, uniform cells, non-invasive) and malignant (invasive, irregular, and metastatic) neoplasms. Note that benign tumors can still be lethal if they grow large enough to compress vital organs.
* **Tissue Origins:** * **Carcinomas:** Epithelial tissue.
* **Sarcomas:** Connective tissue (e.g., Chondrosarcoma in cartilage, Fibrosarcoma in fibrous tissue).
* **Leukemias:** Referred to as "circulating tumors" because they do not form solid masses but are interspersed in the blood and lymphatic system.
* **Lymphomas:** Cancers of the lymphatic system.


* **Paraneoplastic Syndromes:** These are symptoms caused by hormones or antibodies secreted by tumors. Examples include Cushing syndrome (lung/thyroid cancer), Addison syndrome (lymphoma), Hypercalcemia (lung cancer), and Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (leukemia).
* **Etiology:** Risk factors include UV radiation (melanoma), ionizing radiation (leukemia/lung cancer), chemical exposure (vinyl chloride linked to liver cancer), and hormonal factors (nulliparity or lack of children increases ovarian cancer risk).

#### 2. Pharmacological Mechanisms and Specific Agents

* **Folate Antagonists (e.g., Methotrexate, Pemetrexed):** These inhibit dihydrofolate reductase, preventing DNA synthesis. Methotrexate is also used for non-cancerous conditions like rheumatoid arthritis and psoriasis.
* **Pyrimidine Antagonists (e.g., Cytarabine, Fluorouracil, Capecitabine):** These interrupt DNA/RNA synthesis. Cytarabine is specifically used for meningeal leukemia.
* **Purine Antagonists (e.g., Cladribine, Fludarabine):** Cladribine is the drug of choice for hairy cell leukemia, while Fludarabine is used for chronic lymphocytic leukemia.
* **Mitotic Inhibitors:**
* **Vinca Alkaloids (Vincristine):** Dissolve mitotic spindles to inhibit cell division.
* **Taxanes (Paclitaxel):** Form nonfunctional microtubules to halt mitosis.


* **Topoisomerase I Inhibitors (Irinotecan, Topotecan):** Used for metastatic colorectal or small-cell lung cancers.
* **Antineoplastic Enzymes (Asparaginase, Pegaspargase):** Used exclusively for acute lymphocytic leukemia (ALL) by converting asparagine to aspartic acid and ammonia, starving leukemia cells.

#### 3. Nursing Management and Side Effect Mitigation

* **Bone Marrow Suppression (Myelosuppression):**
* **Neutropenia:** Low WBC count (specifically neutrophils <1500 cells/mm³). Monitor for fever (100.5° F), chills, and productive cough. Check temperature every 4 hours.
* **Thrombocytopenia:** Low platelet count. Monitor for petechiae (red/purple spots), bruising, and bleeding.
* **Anemia:** Low RBC count/hemoglobin.


* **Gastrointestinal Care:**
* **Stomatitis:** Inflammation of oral mucosa. Use soft-bristled toothbrushes and warm saline rinses. Avoid alcohol, citrus, spicy foods, and lemon-glycerin swabs. Use sugarless gum for dryness.
* **Nausea/Vomiting:** Administer antiemetics 30–60 minutes *before* chemotherapy. Ginger ale/tea can help. Use IV fluids for severe cases to prevent dehydration.
* **Diarrhea:** Often managed with antidiarrheals, adsorbents, or antisecretory agents.


* **Alopecia:** Reassure patients that hair loss is usually temporary and starts 7–10 days after treatment begins. Suggest wigs or scarves before loss occurs.

### Nuances for Advanced Mastery

#### 1. Critical Safety and Administration Nuances

* **Vincristine Lethality:** This drug is for **IV use only**. Intrathecal administration is fatal. It should be diluted in 25 mL of fluid.
* **Asparaginase Route:** The **intramuscular (IM)** route is preferred over IV or subcutaneous to minimize the risk of clotting abnormalities and organ toxicity.
* **The "Nadir":** This is the point of lowest blood cell counts following chemotherapy (usually 10–28 days). This is when the patient is at the highest risk for infection and bleeding.
* **Methotrexate Rescue:** High-dose methotrexate must be followed by **Leucovorin**. Leucovorin provides the body with active folic acid, "rescuing" healthy cells from death while the drug kills cancer cells.

#### 2. Drug-Drug and Drug-Condition Interactions

* **Methotrexate + NSAIDs/Aspirin:** NSAIDs reduce the renal excretion of methotrexate, leading to toxic accumulation and severe GI toxicity.
* **Capecitabine + Warfarin:** This combination significantly increases the risk of bleeding; warfarin may need to be discontinued.
* **Vincristine + Phenytoin:** Vincristine reduces the effectiveness of phenytoin, increasing seizure risk.
* **Topotecan + Filgrastim:** Do not give these simultaneously. Filgrastim (WBC stimulant) should be given 24 hours *after* chemotherapy is completed.

#### 3. Specific Syndromes and Antidotes

* **Cytarabine Syndrome:** Characterized by fever, muscle pain, malaise, and conjunctivitis. It is treated/prevented with **corticosteroids**.
* **Tumor Lysis Syndrome:** Caused by the rapid breakdown of cancer cells, leading to high uric acid. **Rasburicase** is used to reduce uric acid levels.
* **Irinotecan and "Cholinergic Diarrhea":** This drug can cause severe, life-threatening diarrhea. It must be treated aggressively with **Loperamide**.
* **Pancreatitis:** A major adverse effect of antineoplastic enzymes (Asparaginase/Pegaspargase). Monitor serum alkaline phosphatase and WBC counts closely.

#### 4. Pregnancy and Radiation

* Chemotherapy is most teratogenic during the **first trimester**. During the second and third trimesters, the benefits may outweigh fetal risks.
* **Radiation therapy** is generally contraindicated during pregnancy due to fetal fatality risks but can be administered in the postpartum period.

#### 5. Laboratory and Diagnostic Nuances

* **Prognostic Factors:** The stage of cancer at diagnosis and the efficacy of the initial treatment are the most significant factors in determining a patient's survival length.
* **Neutrophil Threshold:** Chemotherapy like Topotecan should be withheld if the neutrophil count is less than 1500 cells/mm³.
* **Metabolic Toxicity:** Chemotherapy can cause hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, and hypertriglyceridemia.
