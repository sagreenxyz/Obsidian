# Chapter 56, Dermatologic Drugs

Based on the provided student study materials, here are the essential points and nuances for mastering the topic of dermatologic drugs.

### **Main Points for Mastery**

* **Physiologic Functions of the Skin:** The skin serves three primary roles: **protection** (a barrier against microbes and chemicals), **sensation** (transmitting impulses for pain, pressure, and temperature), and **absorption** (diffusion of gases and medications through pores).
* **Acne Medications:**
* **Benzoyl Peroxide:** Kills *P. acnes* by releasing active oxygen. It is a keratolytic but can cause allergic reactions like blistering or swelling, which necessitates stopping treatment.
* **Tretinoin (Vitamin A Acid):** Stimulates epidermal cell turnover. It is used for acne and hyperpigmentation caused by sun damage.
* **Isotretinoin:** Inhibits sebaceous gland activity. It is a high-risk teratogen (Pregnancy Category X), requiring strict contraceptive measures (two methods) and monitoring for signs of depression or suicide.
* **Clindamycin:** A topical antibiotic that may cause local burning, itching, dryness, and peeling.


* **Antifungal Treatments:**
* **Nystatin:** The drug of choice for oral thrush (candidiasis) in infants.
* **Clotrimazole:** A safer option for pregnant women (Pregnancy Category B) compared to other azoles.
* **Miconazole:** Effective for *Tinea versicolor* (scaly, hyperpigmented patches).
* **Butenafine:** Specifically indicated for tinea pedis (athlete’s foot).


* **Psoriasis and Immunomodulators:**
* **Tazarotene:** An antipsoriatic (Pregnancy Category X) that requires a negative pregnancy test within two weeks of starting therapy.
* **Ustekinumab:** Used for plaque psoriasis; it suppresses the immune system, increasing infection risk (monitor WBC counts).
* **Pimecrolimus:** Prescribed for atopic dermatitis (eczema), particularly in patients with a history of asthma or hay fever.


* **Wound and Burn Care:**
* **Silver Sulfadiazine:** Used for preventing infection in 2nd- and 3rd-degree burns. It should be applied to cleansed, debrided wounds using sterile gloves.
* **Neosporin:** A triple-antibiotic combination (neomycin, polymyxin B, and bacitracin) for minor wounds.
* **Povidone-iodine:** A broad-spectrum antiseptic effective against bacteria, viruses, and fungi.


* **Antivirals:** **Acyclovir** must be applied frequently (every 3 hours, 6 times daily). Users must wear gloves during application to prevent the spread of the viral infection.

### **Nuances to Master**

* **Dosage Form Selection:** * **Creams** are nongreasy and best for **moist** areas but should never be used on broken tissue.
* **Ointments** are occlusive and greasy, making them ideal for **dry** lesions.
* **Aerosols** are preferred for burned or tender skin to avoid the pain of physical contact.
* **Lotions** are best for hairy areas or large surface areas.


* **Systemic Absorption of Topicals:** Even topically applied drugs can have systemic effects. For example, **Minoxidil** applied to the scalp can cause tachycardia, fluid retention, and weight gain if absorbed systemically.
* **Topical vs. Systemic Antibiotics for Burns:** Topical antibiotics are often preferred for burns because the destruction of blood vessels in burned tissue prevents systemic antibiotics from reaching the site of action effectively.
* **Hormonal Exacerbations:** A student must recognize that both **Acne vulgaris** and **Eczema** typically worsen during menstruation due to hormonal fluctuations.
* **Herbal Considerations (Aloe):** While popular for wound healing, aloe can cause **renal failure (nephritis)** if used long-term and is contraindicated in patients with kidney disease. It can also cause diarrhea and abdominal pain if ingested.
* **Scabies Management:** Because scabies is highly communicable via direct contact and shared items, **prophylactic treatment** is required for all family members of the infected patient, regardless of whether they show symptoms.
* **Cancer Identification:**
* **Basal Cell Carcinoma:** Arises in the deepest epidermal layer; rarely metastatic.
* **Squamous Cell Carcinoma:** Aggressive and requires surgery or radiation.
* **Actinic Keratosis:** Pre-cancerous sun damage treated with Fluorouracil, which requires strict photosensitivity precautions (sunscreen and protective clothing).
