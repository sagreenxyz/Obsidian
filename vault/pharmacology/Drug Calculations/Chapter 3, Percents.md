# Chapter 3, Percents

To master the topic of percents as presented in this study guide, a student must be proficient in several mathematical conversions and applications, while also paying close attention to specific procedural nuances and test-taking strategies.

### Main Points: Essential Mathematical Operations

**1. Converting Fractions to Percentages**

* **The Rule:** Multiply the fraction by 100 and add the percentage sign (%).
* **Calculation:** $(\text{Numerator} \div \text{Denominator}) \times 100$. For example, $35/70 \times 100 = 50\%$.

**2. Converting Decimals to Percentages**

* **The Rule:** Multiply the decimal by 100 and add the percentage sign.
* **The Shortcut:** Move the decimal point two places to the right. For example, $0.1264$ becomes $12.64\%$.

**3. Converting Percentages to Decimals**

* **The Rule:** Drop the percentage sign and divide the number by 100.
* **The Shortcut:** Move the decimal point two places to the left. If there is no whole number, place a leading zero before the decimal point (e.g., $50\%$ becomes $0.5$).

**4. Converting Mixed Numbers to Percentages**

* **The Process:** 1.  Convert the mixed number into an improper fraction.
2.  Multiply that fraction by 100/1.
3.  Perform the division and add the percentage sign.
* *Example:* $2 \frac{1}{2}$ becomes $5/2$. $5/2 \times 100 = 250\%$.



**5. Finding the Percentage of a Given Number**

* **The Process:**
1. Convert the percentage into a decimal.
2. Multiply that decimal by the target number.


* *Example:* To find $20\%$ of $500$, calculate $0.2 \times 500 = 100$.



**6. Calculating What Percentage One Number is of Another**

* **The Process (Strict Order):**
1. Identify the number following the word "of" and write it as the denominator.
2. Write the other number as the numerator.
3. Divide the numerator by the denominator.
4. Extend the decimal fraction four places to the right of the decimal point.
5. Multiply by 100 and add the percentage sign.



**7. Converting Percentages with Fractions to Decimals**

* **The Process:**
1. Drop the percentage sign.
2. Convert the mixed number to an improper fraction.
3. Divide by 100 (which is the same as multiplying the denominator by 100).
4. Divide the final numerator by the final denominator to get the decimal value.


* *Example:* $10 \frac{1}{2}\%$ becomes $21/2 \div 100 = 21/200 = 0.105$.

### Nuances for Mastery

* **Decimal Precision and Rounding:** Mastery requires strict adherence to rounding instructions provided in the prompt.
* Always check if the answer requires rounding to the **tenth** (one decimal place), **hundredth** (two decimal places), or **thousandth** (three decimal places).
* When calculating a percentage from a fraction, it is standard to divide out to four decimal places before multiplying by 100 to ensure accuracy.


* **Leading Zeros:** For decimals less than 1, a leading zero must always be placed before the decimal point (e.g., $0.12$, not $.12$).
* **Directional Accuracy:** A common error is moving the decimal the wrong way. Remember:
* Decimal to Percent $\rightarrow$ Right (number gets larger).
* Percent to Decimal $\leftarrow$ Left (number gets smaller).


* **Mixed Fraction Conversion:** When converting values like $9 \frac{1}{3}\%$, you must follow the sequence: Improper fraction $\rightarrow$ Multiply by $1/100$ $\rightarrow$ Final division.

### Supplemental Knowledge: Test-Taking Strategies

The study material emphasizes that pharmacology and math competency involve more than just calculation; they require mental preparedness:

* **Calculator Use:** For exams like the NCLEX, be prepared to use an on-screen calculator rather than manual long division.
* **Time Management:** Aim to answer math questions within 1 to 2 minutes. Do not rush, but do not linger so long that you lose momentum.
* **Confidence:** Trust your initial response to a math item, as it is statistically more likely to be correct than a second-guessed answer.
* **Stress Reduction:** Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, shoulder shrugging, or rotating the neck to reduce tension during the examination.
* **Active Preparation:** Familiarize yourself with computer-based testing interfaces and use study groups to "think like the test-maker" by creating practice questions.
